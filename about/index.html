About

Herman Stenko is a London-based fashion designer and educator. A Central Saint Martins graduate in Fashion Design and Marketing, he is currently completing an MA in Fashion Marketing at London College of Fashion.

His work is craft-led and material-driven, rooted in pattern cutting, construction, and experimentation with texture and reclaimed fabric. He first gained recognition as a winner of the LVMH Nona Source project, working alongside designers from Louis Vuitton, Dior, and Loewe on sustainability and fabric innovation, and has since collaborated with Alexander McQueen and designed at Charles Jeffrey LOVERBOY.

Alongside his practice, he is a dedicated educator. With over four years of teaching across Central Saint Martins, London College of Fashion, and international programmes, he guides students through the full creative process — from first research to finished portfolio — helping them shape a design identity that is unmistakably their own.
